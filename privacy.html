Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Grapebook is a personal wine tasting journal. This policy explains what data the app collects, why, and what your choices are. The short version: your journal is yours, we don't sell data, and there are no ads or third-party trackers.

What we collect

  • Account details — your email address and, optionally, your first and last name and a username. You can also use Grapebook as a guest without an account; guest notes stay on your device.
  • Your journal — the tasting notes you create, including any photos of wine labels you add.
  • Social activity — friend connections, notes you choose to share, comments and likes. Sharing is always opt-in.
  • Purchase status — if you subscribe to Grapebook Pro, our payment partner RevenueCat processes the purchase through Apple. We never see your payment details.
  • Push token & language — if you enable notifications, a device push token; and your app language, so notifications arrive in your language.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, no ad networks, no third-party analytics trackers.
  • No selling or renting of personal data — ever.
  • No public profile unless you explicitly share a note with the community.

AI label scanning

When you scan a wine label, the photo is sent to an AI service (Google Gemini) solely to read the label and return the wine details. The photo is not used to train models and is not stored by us beyond your own note.

Where your data lives

Your account and journal are stored with Supabase, our database provider, protected by row-level security so only you (and people you explicitly share with) can access your notes.

Deleting your data

You can delete your account at any time from within the app (Account → Delete account). This permanently removes your account, tasting notes, photos, friends and shares. This action cannot be undone.
